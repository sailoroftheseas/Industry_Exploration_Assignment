Introduction

I think the company that most excites/interests me is apple. Since I was about 14 I started to gain interest into their technology. I fell in love with their design and teachnology, so I started teaching myself coding through books and online guides, youtube tutorials, and websites like stack exchange. For the next few years I started making iOS apps for the iphone/ipad. I love the simplicity and sleekness of their design. From apple's computers to their website, everything is of high quality and works seamlessly. Apple's clean, intuitive, uncluttered, and seamless design is captivating. Additionally, Apple’s design practices are incredibly unique and innovative.

What it would be like to work for Apple

If I could get a job at apple that would be a dream come true. I imagine the environment would be a relaxed workspace, where everyone is friendly and communicates ideas and concepts well together, even if theyre from different departments. Collaborating with other intelligent and innovative developers would be great. If you work together in a cooperation and friendly way you can accomplish great things. This would be an ideal situation for me. It's all about effective and mindful communication. It also involves constructive criticism and honesty between co workers/collaborators. I like the thought of that environment. To get a job at apple I think you would have to show them your intense dedication, enthusiasm, honesty, meticulous work, creativity, and an eye for design. I think working at apple would take a lot of work, long hours, precision and detail, thinking out of the box, and thoroughness in your work/projects. I would want to either create games, website development, graphic design that applies to websites, computer os, mobile apps, or front end software dev (and maybe a little backend development too). I think working there would be a fun, creative experience solving complex problems and developing simple yet powerful applications. Also, website development would be interesting as well as creating the graphics to go with the software/apps/games.
